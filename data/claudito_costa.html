Professor do Instituto de História e no Programa de Pós-Graduação em História Social da UFRJ, Brasil (desde 2016). Minhas atividades de pesquisa se desenvolvem na interface entre história e antropologia, principalmente na investigação sobre desenvolvimento, colonialismo e institucionalização do poder, entre contextos do Sul Global. Também observo as mesmas questões em relação a uma economia política e sociologia intelectual da produção de conhecimento, analisando a produção científica internacional desenvolvida fora de centros hegemônicos. Associo as duas chaves de pesquisa a partir da análise dos efeitos duráveis do colonialismo, materializados no desenvolvimento de agendas temáticas, semântica, vocabulários, quadros teóricos e epistemológicos das ciências humanas contemporâneas.Deliberadamente escolho produzir em línguas cientificamente não-hegemônicas (português, espanhol, chinês, árabe, coreano e bengalês), bem como em inglês ou francês, a fim de consolidar redes trans-regionais e integrar meu trabalho aos debates que acontecem nesses diferentes ambientes intelectuais. Além do trabalho como pesquisador desenvolvo um trabalho institucional como diretor do Programa Sephis (Holanda), dedicada a construção de capacidades científicas e à promoção da produção de conhecimento no Sul Global. Tenho sido professor e/ou pesquisador associado e visitante em diversos países, com uma atuação ativa na docência, pesquisa e atividades de política científica (nos US) Wellesley College/Harvard University (2020-2019); (na Europa) Vrije Universität (Amsterdam), Wien Universität (Vienna, 2019), Universität zu Köln (Colônia, 2019-2017), Universidade Humboldt (2016-2015), Freie Universität (Berlin, 2013-2012); Universidade de Lisboa (2003); (na Índia) Universidade de Calcutá (2005), de Goa (2006), de Delhi (2005 e 2012), e no Center for the Study of Developing Societies (2005), e na Universidade Bukkyo, em Kyoto, no Japão (2001-2). Foi bolsista de diversas fundações e entidades filantrópicas no Brasil (CNPq, da CAPES, FAPERJ, FAPESP, FGV, Ministério da Educação) e no Exterior (Social Science Research Council, Norbert Elias Foundation, Volkswagen Stiftung, Ford Foundation, Sephis Programme, Ministério da Educação da Alemanha, do Instituto Internacional de Estudos Asiáticos e da UNESCO). Laboratório de Leituras e Conexões Sul-Sul : LACSS - IH 1553 ECMR T01 Este laboratório estrutura-se a partir da proposta de constituição de um núcleo de debates e leituras sobre Teorias Sociais e Epistemologias desenvolvidas fora de contextos hegemônicos das ciências humanas, o chamado ?Sul?. Assim, partimos da leitura sistemática de formulações intelectuais ulteriormente ocupadas em reverter a tendência histórica de dependência de contextos periféricos (em Índia, México, Cingapura, Brasil, África do Sul, Senegal e Oriente Médio) em relação ao main stream acadêmico internacional. O curso configura-se em um primeiro espaço institucionalizado para a discussão de uma literatura muito pouco visível e visitada no Brasil. Pretendemos que seja ainda um movimento introdutório para um debate que se quer transformar em uma disciplina regular formal.

Ethics in collaborative scientific research
Descrição: Ethics in science encompasses a broad spectrum of discussions spanning various scientific fields, from biology and medicine to Social and Human Sciences. While much attention has been devoted to ethical considerations in the researcher-to-object relationship, particularly in life sciences involving experiments with humans or animals, there remains a significant gap in understanding the ethical intricacies within the internal dynamics of research, within teams and institutions. This work address these gaps, shedding light on the following aspects: (i) interactions among scholars, by examining how inequalities manifest on collaborations between scholars from diverse academic and social and identity backgrounds, at different stages of their professional and personal development. (ii) structural asymmetries, by investigating intersectional inequalities related to race, gender, sexuality, positionality, class, caste; (iii) impact of prestige and reputation, by analyzing the influence of factors such as prestige or reputation, on the internal dynamics of working groups, exploring how these factors can contribute promote and reinforce pervasive biases and forms of exclusion; (iii) power dynamics within working groups, by exploring situations where power dynamics within research teams significantly affect the production of knowledge in terms of unequal distribution of resources, funding and prestige. This research combined theoretical discussions and historical work with the detailed analysis of a some of collaborative projects on the Indian Ocean funded by SEPHIS Programme, between 2009-2011 and other funded by the Social Science Research Council, in 2020-2022. This initiative, driven by the shared concerns of SSRC and Sephis regarding equality and ethics in science, explored the challenges and benefits of collaborative research, focusing on dimensions of inequality normally overlooked by structural analyses. The research delves into nuanced aspects such as prestige, reputation, certification, accreditation and their roles in inflating or jeopardizing scientific careers. The goal is to enhance our understanding of ethical considerations beyond traditional frameworks, fostering a more comprehensive approach to ethics in collaborative scientific research..

North and South: Forms of Inequality Within International Politics of Scientific Production
Descrição: The debate around the North-South divide, as an expression of a global structure of durable inequalities, accumulates a significant amount of literature since the late 1960s. In the West, the discussion originally referred to spatial representations of wealth and progress, therefore addressing Geographies of Economic Inequality. In recent years, the North-South gap has led to a continuum of intellectual dependency affecting the international political economy of knowledge production and circulation. In this context, most contributions concentrate on the weight of North-Atlantic hegemonic agendas for Humanities at large (theories, vocabulary of analytical categories, methodologies and, fundamentally, epistemologies). For those, the North-South division represents Geographies of Epistemic Inequality. As a concept addressing spatial representations of inequality, the South is assumedly relevant for analyzing issues affecting peripheral societies (taken as a derivative effect of the expansion of colonialism and capitalism), but not having much to say about issues considered relevant in societies labeled as ?Northern?. Can the concept of the South speak to other aspects of social life outside the framework of development disparities as those concerning the constitution of the self, for instance? As for knowledge production, the South has been suitable to stress the importance of diversity in Humanities, but with presumably less resonance in Natural/Pure Sciences. Can the term help challenge the basic Natural Sciences versus Humanities slit, referring to science and societies at large? Our symposium focuses on the heuristic potential of the South to critically observe how hegemonic agendas in science and funding priorities (state or private) impact the lack of diversity in knowledge as a whole taken from the importance of theoretical diversity, intellectual creativity, gender balance, the role of language, the variety of ontological beings and forms of conviviality, and others, impacting the present world..

Descrição: A chamada Teoria da Dependência propunha uma leitura crítica acerca dos processos de desenvolvimento de Estados Nacionais na América Latina. Houve um investimento de natureza complementar em dois motes. Descrição: De par com o processo de expansão colonialista dos Estados europeus modernos, foram deflagradas diversas movimentações no mundo então conhecido. Destarte foi que dicionários e outros instrumentos de tradução foram usados para criar inteligibilidade entre línguas, mas também entre culturas, costumes e formas de sociabilidade ? na Europa, assim como nas terras conquistadas. Entre os objetivos dessa investigação está a perspectiva de recolocar questões à uma agenda internacional de debates sobre colonialismo e pós-colonialismo, a partir da reconstrução das trajetórias e de iniciativas que terminaram ensejando políticas coloniais portuguesas ligadas a tradução e codificação de línguas em Ásia e América do Sul, a partir dos alvores da Modernidade..

Traduzindo mundos, inventando impérios
Descrição: De par com o processo de expansão colonialista dos Estados europeus modernos foram deflagradas diversas movimentações no mundo então conhecido. Passaram a existir intensos fluxos de produtos, espécies (de fauna e flora) e pessoas. No que tange a circulação de pessoas, a compreensão de seus costumes e realidades, um dos dilemas que cedo se instalou foi a dificuldade de compreensão das línguas. A galáxia das línguas que foi sendo contatada era imensa e o próprio avanço e sucesso da empreitada colonialista dependeu da capacidade de estabelecer contatos. Em um primeiro momento, recorreu-se aos línguas (tradutores) enquanto mediadores da comunicação entre europeus e demais grupos contatados. Simultaneamente, ocorria na Europa um intenso movimento de civilização (domesticação) dos vernáculos a partir de fórmulas gramaticais baseadas no latim. Num curto espaço de tempo após o início da gramatização e dicionarização das línguas européias, o mesmo ocorreu entre as línguas sul-americanas, asiáticas e, posteriormente, africanas. Os dicionários (de par com gramáticas, ortografias e afins, além dos catecismos) surgem daí como textos de tradução foram livros usados para criar inteligibilidade entre línguas, mas também entre culturas, costumes e formas de sociabilidade. Nosso objetivo é perceber, a partir deste mote, como categorias sociais ligadas ao universo do exercício do poder (das formas de dominação), nos diversos contextos coloniais eram percebidas, traduzidas e re-significadas a partir de categorias lingüísticas (léxicos) européias, no caso, do português..

Ethics in collaborative scientific research
Descrição: Ethics in science encompasses a broad spectrum of discussions spanning various scientific fields, from biology and medicine to Social and Human Sciences. While much attention has been devoted to ethical considerations in the researcher-to-object relationship, particularly in life sciences involving experiments with humans or animals, there remains a significant gap in understanding the ethical intricacies within the internal dynamics of research, within teams and institutions. This work address these gaps, shedding light on the following aspects: (i) interactions among scholars, by examining how inequalities manifest on collaborations between scholars from diverse academic and social and identity backgrounds, at different stages of their professional and personal development. (ii) structural asymmetries, by investigating intersectional inequalities related to race, gender, sexuality, positionality, class, caste; (iii) impact of prestige and reputation, by analyzing the influence of factors such as prestige or reputation, on the internal dynamics of working groups, exploring how these factors can contribute promote and reinforce pervasive biases and forms of exclusion; (iii) power dynamics within working groups, by exploring situations where power dynamics within research teams significantly affect the production of knowledge in terms of unequal distribution of resources, funding and prestige. This research combined theoretical discussions and historical work with the detailed analysis of a some of collaborative projects on the Indian Ocean funded by SEPHIS Programme, between 2009-2011 and other funded by the Social Science Research Council, in 2020-2022. This initiative, driven by the shared concerns of SSRC and Sephis regarding equality and ethics in science, explored the challenges and benefits of collaborative research, focusing on dimensions of inequality normally overlooked by structural analyses. The research delves into nuanced aspects such as prestige, reputation, certification, accreditation and their roles in inflating or jeopardizing scientific careers. The goal is to enhance our understanding of ethical considerations beyond traditional frameworks, fostering a more comprehensive approach to ethics in collaborative scientific research..

North and South: Forms of Inequality Within International Politics of Scientific Production
Descrição: The debate around the North-South divide, as an expression of a global structure of durable inequalities, accumulates a significant amount of literature since the late 1960s. In the West, the discussion originally referred to spatial representations of wealth and progress, therefore addressing Geographies of Economic Inequality. In recent years, the North-South gap has led to a continuum of intellectual dependency affecting the international political economy of knowledge production and circulation. In this context, most contributions concentrate on the weight of North-Atlantic hegemonic agendas for Humanities at large (theories, vocabulary of analytical categories, methodologies and, fundamentally, epistemologies). For those, the North-South division represents Geographies of Epistemic Inequality. As a concept addressing spatial representations of inequality, the South is assumedly relevant for analyzing issues affecting peripheral societies (taken as a derivative effect of the expansion of colonialism and capitalism), but not having much to say about issues considered relevant in societies labeled as ?Northern?. Can the concept of the South speak to other aspects of social life outside the framework of development disparities as those concerning the constitution of the self, for instance? As for knowledge production, the South has been suitable to stress the importance of diversity in Humanities, but with presumably less resonance in Natural/Pure Sciences. Can the term help challenge the basic Natural Sciences versus Humanities slit, referring to science and societies at large? Our symposium focuses on the heuristic potential of the South to critically observe how hegemonic agendas in science and funding priorities (state or private) impact the lack of diversity in knowledge as a whole taken from the importance of theoretical diversity, intellectual creativity, gender balance, the role of language, the variety of ontological beings and forms of conviviality, and others, impacting the present world..
